What is Astrophysics ?

Astrophysics is the branch of astronomy that applies the principles of physics and chemistry to understand the universe and its objects, such as stars, planets, and galaxies. It focuses on the "how" and "why" behind what astronomers observe, using physical laws to explain phenomena like star formation, black holes, and the origins and evolution of the cosmos. Astrophysicists use a combination of observational data from telescopes and computational models to develop theories about the universe. Modern astrophysics research grapples with major mysteries, including the nature of dark matter and dark energy, the mechanisms behind stellar explosions like supernovae, the formation and evolution of galaxies, and the potential for life on exoplanets.
